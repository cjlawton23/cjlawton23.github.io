Six signs it's time to Redesign Your Website!

Posted by Casey Lawton on July 8, 2015

Websites can be costly and especially for small businesses it can be hard to justify the expense in hiring a good web developer. However, in today's world it is absolutely critical for a business to have a modern website. Customers expect it and when they see a website that looks like it's from 2008 most of the time they’ll turn right around and leave. So is it time to redesign your web page? Here’s 5 ways to tell.

  1. Responsive / Mobile friendly design

    2. Responsive websites dynamically adjust to all of the different screen sizes on all of the various devices the modern world uses now. A true responsive website doesn’t reload a separate mobile site but adjust on the fly. Why should you care? Check out some of these statisitcs below.

    • 61% of people have a better opinion of brands when they offer a good mobile experience.
    • 25.85% of all emails are opened on mobile phones, and 10.16% are opened on tablets.
    • Over 29% of Google searches are performed on a mobile device
    • According to Google’s Think Insights on mobile, if a user lands on your mobile website and is frustrated or doesn’t see what they are looking for, there’s a 61% chance they will leave immediately and go to another website (most likely a competitor). It’s also said that if they have a positive experience with your mobile website, a user is 67% more likely to buy a product or use a service.
    • Social networks bring in users from mobile

  2. Social media incorporation

    3. If your site hasn’t incorporated social media yet then it’s definitely time for a redesign. Don’t use social media? You should. However, even if you don’t you can still incorporate methods for your users to easily like and share your content with their friends on their social, bringing in even more customers. Like the socail buttons at the bottom of this article! :)

  3. SEO

    4. Are you coming up in google search results? Does google even know you exist? SEO is critical for your customers finding you on the web. Google’s search engine algorithms have changed significantly over the years, has your sites SEO?

  4. Old Copyright

    5. If your site hasn’t been updated in years or has a copyright date that’s not the current years date you could very well be turning away customers. When your customer visits your site and see that it hasn’t been given any attention in years they immediately think, “Why should I?”

  5. Analytics

    6. Another big one. If you haven’t integrated some kind of analytics software like Google analytics you're really hurting yourself. Analytics tell you who your customer is, when they’re visiting, how long they’re staying and if they are returning, along with much more. Without analytic tracking you can’t tell if your site is growing or declining.

  6. Modern Design

    7. The world of web design is constantly evolving. What was considered modern just 4-5 years ago can be considered dated today. Current modern design incorporates big high resolution beautiful images, great fonts, minimalist design, subtle animations, as well as having all of the above 5 features.
